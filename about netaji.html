Subhas Chandra Bose

  1. Born:-

    Subhas Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, British India, to Prabhavati Dutt Bose and Janakinath Bose.

  2. Education:-

    Bose received his early education at Protestant European School and Ravenshaw Collegiate School in Cuttack. He excelled academically and was deeply influenced by Swami Vivekananda's teachings during his school years.Bose later attended Presidency College in Calcutta (now Kolkata), where he studied under the influence of nationalist teachers. He was known for his exceptional academic performance and his involvement in student politics.

  3. Civil Service Exams:-

    Bose appeared for the Indian Civil Service (ICS) examinations in 1920 and stood fourth in the order of merit. However, he resigned from the coveted position in protest against the oppressive British rule following the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919.

  4. Entry into Politics:-

    Bose became actively involved in the Indian National Congress and the independence movement.He was deeply inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's ideologies and methods of nonviolent resistance. Bose rose through the ranks of the Indian National Congress quickly, becoming the president of the All India Youth Congress and the Bengal Congress. His radical approach and strong belief in direct action sometimes brought him into conflict with the more moderate leaders of the Congress.

  5. Presidency of Indian National Congress:-

    Bose was elected as the president of the Indian National Congress in 1938 and again in 1939. However, his differences with the Congress leadership, particularly with Gandhi and Jawaharlal Nehru, led to his resignation from the presidency in 1939.

  6. The Indian National Army (INA):-

    Bose established the Indian National Army (INA) in 1942 in Southeast Asia with the support of Indian prisoners of war and expatriates. The INA played a significant role in the struggle against British rule in India during World War II.

  7. Lost The Hero:-

    Bose's fate remains a matter of debate. It's widely believed that he died in a plane crash in Taiwan on August 18, 1945. However, conspiracy theories suggest that he may have survived and lived in secrecy after the war.
